# Hero Vision Reference

These images (`apps/personal/public/hero-vision-desktop.jpg` and `hero-vision-mobile.jpg`) are the northstar for the portfolio landing hero. They are not final production assets — they encode the tone, density, and proof-first layout the live site should achieve. Use them when implementing the hero section, choosing typography, and deciding what belongs above the fold.

The hero sells **Edd Norris** as a senior builder whose work spans environmental systems and interactive media. Proof is visible immediately: torn parchment intro with the headline *"I love building solutions, for fun and function,"* two practice portals on a tablet, a living work map, a career timeline strip, identity badges, and a collaboration sticky note. Nothing is decorative filler — every object is a navigable surface or credibility signal.

## Design principles encoded

**Dense, purposeful desk — not empty wallpaper.** The composition is a creator-studio flat-lay on warm wood, with golden-hour Pacific Northwest light through a country window (Oregon forest, distant mountain). Objects overlap and layer; the frame feels lived-in and worked-in, not a blurred 80% background with a headline floating on top.

**Real product feel, cinematic polish.** UI on tablets should look like shippable interfaces (practice portals, work map graph, timeline cards), not stock-photo laptop mockups. Analog texture (torn cream paper, compass, mug, plant, polaroids) grounds the digital work. Day mode is preferred for the hero: moody warmth without going full night-mode.

**Mobile stacks the same story.** On phone, the parchment intro and headline lead, practice portals stack vertically, and the work map / timeline / badges compress below — same proof, tighter vertical rhythm. Navigation collapses to name + menu; identity badges become a bottom skills bar.
